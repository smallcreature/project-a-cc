# Project Arcane MVP Concept

## Summary

Source describes [[entities/Project Arcane|Project Arcane]] as a survival-craft game about discovering magic through research, experimentation, resource logistics, and systemic consequences. Core promise: player starts with harsh manual survival, then uses [[concepts/Magic System|magic]] to simplify, automate, and eventually break survival-craft rules.

Design focuses on:

- [[concepts/Core USP|strong USP]] through complex magic, deep specialization, changing world, PvE hunters, and co-op roles.
- [[concepts/Core Gameplay Loop|core loop]] where research drives expeditions, expeditions unlock resources, resources unlock crafting, and crafting unlocks stronger research/exploration.
- [[concepts/Survival Systems|survival systems]] deep enough to matter early, but deliberately softened or bypassed by magic later.
- [[concepts/Aberration And Corruption|corruption]] as consequence and opportunity: unsafe magic changes regions but unlocks rare resources and power.
- [[concepts/Deep Co-op|deep co-op]] through role specialization across magic schools, farming, study, mining, infrastructure, and combat.

## Key Claims

- Manual friction is intentional early because later magical automation feels powerful only if the player first felt the cost of doing work manually.
- The main fantasy is not just "being a mage", but being a magic researcher discovering unclear rules.
- Many survival systems are familiar, but their depth and cross-links create emergent gameplay.
- Magic is strongest when it affects all layers: combat, survival, infrastructure, logistics, crafting, exploration, and world state.
- Corruption should be both risk and reward, not only punishment.
- Co-op engagement rises when specialization branches are deep enough that players can meaningfully divide responsibilities.

# Cooking

## Definition

Cooking converts raw food into stronger, longer-lasting, or buff-providing meals and drinks.

## Why It Matters

Cooking turns survival upkeep into progression. Better food increases free-time buffer and can later support mana, movement, research, and combat.

## Details

Methods:

- Roasting: campfire slot, active fire, one ingredient -> one portion; overcooks into spoiled/burned food.
- Boiling: pot on campfire, water required, combines ingredients, can produce up to five portions.
- Drying: slow preservation without fuel; lower satiety, longer shelf life.
- Baking: flour + milk + egg -> dough -> bread in oven; not MVP.
- Juices/wine: press berries into juice; spoiled juice becomes wine.

Workbench tiers:

- T1: campfire, pot.
- T2: quern, dryer.
- T3: press, oven.
- T4: kitchen.

Complex cooking combines raw ingredients and prepared foods/drinks. Advanced meals can grant strong buffs: HP regen, stamina, speed, research chance, mana bonuses, and overfilled hunger/thirst.

Spoiled food still has uses as fertilizer or magic/alchemy ingredient.

Magic can:

- Add mana restoration or buffs.
- Slow spoilage.
- Make cooking portable or built into player inventory.
- Automate simple and complex cooking.

# Spellcasting

## Definition

Spellcasting applies spell effects by spending mana, aura energies, and material aspects, modified by casting method.

## Why It Matters

It is the direct expression of player magical power and a major carrier of the mage-researcher fantasy.

## Details

Casting methods:

- Hands: standard materials, mana, aura energy.
- Wand: no primordial aspects.
- Staff: no aspects; only one spell bound to staff.
- Orb: no aura energy; uses stored internal energy and needs refill.
- Grimoire: no aspects; holds many spells.
- Scroll: no requirements; one-use; hard to produce.
- Ritual: no requirements; stronger effect; needs ritual site and long reading.

Spell research uses an inscriber workbench and drag-and-drop formula mini-game:

- Circle 1: arcane manipulator, defines behavior.
- Circle 2: primordial aspects, defines element and material cost.
- Circle 3: artificial aspects, advanced meaning and cost.

Arcane manipulators:

- Projectile.
- Self.
- Target.
- Area.
- Spawn.

MVP schools:

- Pyromancy.
- Hydromancy.
- Geomancy.
- Aeromancy.
- Druidism.
- Conjuration.
- Chronotopy.
- Aberrationism.
- Arcana.

Spell directions:

- Combat.
- Movement.
- Resources.

Formula UI should give hints once a valid recipe prefix is detected: highlight required slots, correct modules, and wrong modules.

# Aberration And Corruption

## Definition

Aberration is a magical energy generated by magic use. Corruption is its world manifestation: hostile anomalies, terrain change, monsters, flora, fauna, and biome transformation.

## Why It Matters

Corruption is a central consequence system. It makes magic reshape the world and creates risk/reward decisions: cleanse for safety or study corruption for power.

## Details

Most magical actions add aberrational energy to the local [[concepts/Aura And Magical Energies|aura]]. Aberration fills aura capacity and can displace other energies.

Manifestations:

- Rifts damaging nearby players.
- Monsters near rifts.
- Corrupted terrain.
- Corrupted flora.
- Corrupted animal variants.

Corruption stages:

- 15%: aggressive monsters appear globally.
- 25%: damaging rifts spawn.
- 35%: rifts sometimes spawn special monsters.
- 60%: rifts become more visible, alter terrain and resources, spawn corruption flora that can generate more pollution.
- 70%: wild animals inside corrupted blocks may become corrupted variants.
- 90%+: region nearly fully changed, normal mobs stop spawning, spreaders start polluting neighboring regions.

Counterplay:

- Cleansing plants.
- Rituals destroying aberrational energy.
- Buildings monitoring or cleansing corruption.
- Aura manipulation infrastructure.

Opportunity:

- Aberration magic direction.
- Rare resources.
- Stronger spells/items/buildings.
- Riskier playstyle with higher rewards.

## Tensions / Contradictions

- Corruption must threaten the player but also be attractive enough that risky players choose to study it.
- Global monster trigger at 15% may be harsh if any one region can affect the whole world.

## Open Questions

- Are corruption percentages per region, global, or both?
- How reversible is each corruption stage?
- How much warning does player receive before major stage transitions?
